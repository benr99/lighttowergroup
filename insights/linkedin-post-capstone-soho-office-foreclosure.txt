On a Tuesday in August, Joshua Zamir’s Capstone Equities filed a lawsuit in New York State court. The target: Richard Wagman’s Madison Capital and Vornado Realty Trust, co-owners of 140 Crosby Street. Capstone alleged the borrowers defaulted on a $75 million loan that matured in September 2024. The firm had acquired that loan from Société Générale just one month earlier. Less than a year later, Capstone owns the building outright. An affiliate purchased the 35,000-square-foot property from Madison Capital for $51.4 million, per the Commercial Observer. That is $1,469 per square foot—a steep discount from the $100 million asking price the partners floated in summer 2024.
