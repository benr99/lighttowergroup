# Light Tower Editorial Governance

This is the current operating standard for automated Insights and Ideas work.
It applies whether a draft is generated by an AI model, deterministic tooling,
or a human-assisted workflow.

## Publication standard

Every public article must be useful to a sophisticated sponsor, lender, or
investor without requiring a search visit to justify its existence. A strong
piece contributes at least one of the following:

- a specific underwriting implication;
- a primary-source fact, filing, or data point;
- an original Light Tower framing of capital structure or execution risk;
- a decision-useful comparison, timeline, or visual explanation.

Publishing volume is never a quality goal by itself.

## Automated gates

Before a draft can publish, the pipeline applies deterministic checks that do
not trust a writer model's self-assessment:

1. source material is treated as untrusted and instruction-like text is removed;
2. fallback and fixture-source content is held, never published;
3. title similarity is checked against the existing public library;
4. word count, section structure, source validity, boilerplate, and paragraph
   repetition are checked independently;
5. secrets, placeholder text, mojibake, unsupported figures, allegations, and
   speculative writing trigger a hold;
6. public manifests, feeds, and sitemaps are validated before deployment.

## Editorial review triggers

Hold an article for editorial review when it contains legal allegations,
personal claims, a thin source record, material forecasts, a source-quality
concern, a near duplicate, or a claim that would affect a financial decision.

The `data/ideas/held/` directory is an editorial queue, not a failure bin.
Review held reasons weekly and use them to improve source selection and prompts.

## Provenance

Each new Ideas article records source URLs, supplied facts, generated time,
quality/risk signals, and run metadata. Do not remove those records merely to
make a draft publishable. Correct the source record or hold the piece.

## Publishing hygiene

- Use one canonical article per story and angle.
- Redirect retired duplicates; remove them from the hub, feed, and sitemap.
- Publish clear author attribution, dates, sources, and a descriptive social image.
- Keep LinkedIn packages in review unless explicit auto-posting is requested.
- Run `scripts/run_quality_checks.ps1` with the approved execution policy before
  a release or use the equivalent commands in CI.
- Install `scripts/requirements.lock` for a reproducible validated runtime;
  update it deliberately after dependency upgrades.
