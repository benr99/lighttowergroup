## DIRECTION 1: More Operator/Insider Voice
### (Assumes readers understand capital markets deeply)

**System Prompt Change:**
Add to VOICE section:
```
- Skip the basics. Audience knows what DSCR is, what LTV means, 
  why basis matters. Go straight to the capital move.
- Name lenders, sponsors, terms specifically.
- Capital incentive first: Why is this person/lender actually doing this?
- Assume sophisticated underwriting. No hand-holding.
```

**Thread Post 1 Would Become:**
```
Institutional capital is financing mixed-use again. But not like 2019.
Now they're underwriting the operator's ability to hold density and margins 
across retail and residential.

Tuscan Village proved it: Whole Foods is your denominator (predictable cash flow). 
Free People is your numerator (margin capture). The lender's math is: can this 
operator keep foot traffic converting to residential premiums?
```

**Thread Post 5 Would Become:**
```
Here's where this gets real for lenders: the basis now depends entirely on execution.
A 2% DSCR from rent alone won't cut it. Lenders need 1.2x on rent + 0.8x on ancillary 
(events, parking, residential premiums). 

Mess up the placemaking, miss the residential absorption, and the whole stack collapses.
That's new underwriting discipline.
```

**Carousel Slide 1 Would Become:**
```
Headline: "Capital Markets Just Repriced Mixed-Use Risk"

Subhead: Lenders stopped financing on rent per sq ft. They're financing on 
whether the operator can keep foot traffic converting. Four new tenants at 
Tuscan Village? That's just the proof-of-concept lenders demanded.
```

**Carousel Slide 4 Would Become:**
```
Headline: "If Placemaking Fails, the Whole Capital Stack Fails"

Subhead: Old underwriting: Is the tenant creditworthy?
New underwriting: Can the operator program space and keep density?
It's a completely different risk profile. Lenders know it. Sponsors need to know it.
```

## DIRECTION 2: More Provocative/Contrarian Voice
### (Lead with what everyone's missing, bold takes)

**System Prompt Change:**
Add to VOICE section:
```
- Open with the contrarian framing. What's the conventional wisdom? 
  Now tell people why that's wrong or incomplete.
- Use provocative language when justified by data: 
  "Sponsors without this are done." "Lenders are making a mistake." 
  "This is the beginning of the end for..."
- Debate-sparking: End with questions that make people uncomfortable.
```

**Thread Post 1 Would Become:**
```
Everyone thinks Tuscan Village proves mixed-use is back.
They're wrong.

It proves institutional capital will ONLY finance mixed-use if the operator 
can guarantee density and defensible residential premiums.
One deal with the right anchor and the right execution isn't a trend—it's a gatekeep.
```

**Thread Post 5 Would Become:**
```
This is the part that keeps lenders awake: placemaking execution risk is now the 
ENTIRE deal risk.

Bad sponsor? Bad operator? Bad programming? You're not selling at 2019 basis. 
You're selling at a 20% discount. Maybe lower.

Lenders are pricing in what most sponsors haven't realized yet: this operator's 
ability to execute is worth more than the location.
```

**Carousel Slide 1 Would Become:**
```
Headline: "Tuscan Village Isn't a Win for Mixed-Use. It's a Gatekeep."

Subhead: Institutional capital came back—but only for projects that meet 
a specific checklist: anchor tenant that drives daily traffic, operator with 
execution track record, location with residential demand. Tuscan Village won. 
Everything else might be out of luck.
```

**Carousel Slide 4 Would Become:**
```
Headline: "The Sponsor Gap Just Got Wider"

Subhead: Operators who can program space and deliver residential premiums? 
Fully financed. Operators betting on rent alone? Trying to fundraise like it's 2021.
The capital market is segregating. Fast.
```

## DIRECTION 3: More Direct/Tactical (How To) Voice
### (Frame as actionable guidance for operators/lenders)

**System Prompt Change:**
Add to VOICE section:
```
- Write to the operator/lender in the room solving this right now.
- Imperatives are natural: "Here's what you should ask." "If you're underwriting this, track this metric."
- Practical examples from actual underwriting or capital conversations.
- "If you're seeing X, that means Y is probably happening."
```

**Thread Post 1 Would Become:**
```
If you're underwriting a mixed-use development right now, here's what's changed:
Lenders don't care about rent assumptions anymore. They care about your 
foot-traffic model and your retail-to-residential conversion thesis.

Tuscan Village just proved it: can you attract (Whole Foods density) and convert 
(Free People margin)? Then we'll finance you.
```

**Thread Post 5 Would Become:**
```
Here's the practical underwriting question lenders are asking now:
If your retail anchors underperform by 20%, can your residential still hit IRR?

If the answer is "no," you're missing a revenue stream the lender needs to see.
If the answer is "yes," you've got the capital stack.

What's YOUR answer if you're underwriting this deal?
```

**Carousel Slide 1 Would Become:**
```
Headline: "How to Underwrite Mixed-Use in 2026: The Density Gatekeep"

Subhead: Lenders want to see three things: (1) A daily-traffic anchor (Whole Foods). 
(2) An operator with execution credibility. (3) A residential absorption model that 
survives 20% retail underperformance. Tuscan Village shows all three. Yours?
```

**Carousel Slide 4 Would Become:**
```
Headline: "The Underwriting Question You Need to Answer"

Subhead: Ask yourself: If placemaking fails and residential absorption drops 30%, 
can we still hit 1.1x DSCR on rent alone? If the answer is no, lenders will ask for 
more equity. You need that answer before you pitch.
```

## DIRECTION 4: More Conversational/Personal Voice
### (Like texting a colleague about a deal, not writing for a platform)

**System Prompt Change:**
Add to VOICE section:
```
- Contractions, casual language. "What I'm seeing" not "What one might observe."
- Personal experience when relevant: "In my deals we're seeing..." 
- Shorter sentences. One thought per line when possible.
- Humor is fine when grounded in truth.
- Like you're texting/calling a sponsor or lender you know.
```

**Thread Post 1 Would Become:**
```
So Whole Foods and Free People just signed at Tuscan Village.

Here's the thing most people aren't saying out loud: this isn't about rent. 
This is about density. Lenders are finally asking sponsors: "Can you keep 
people in this place long enough that they'll pay a premium for housing nearby?"

Tuscan Village says yes. That changes underwriting.
```

**Thread Post 5 Would Become:**
```
Real talk: placemaking execution is now where deals live or die.

Nail the experience—retail, programming, density—and your residential premiums 
cover everything. Screw it up and you're refinancing at a 15% haircut.

Lenders know it. They're pricing for execution risk now, not tenant risk.
If you're a sponsor, that's your new job: prove you can execute the experience.
```

**Carousel Slide 1 Would Become:**
```
Headline: "Lenders Just Started Caring About Placemaking"

Subhead: Tuscan Village's latest: Whole Foods for daily density, Free People 
for residential lift. The old question "Is the rent good?" is dead. The new 
question: "Can this operator actually run a place?" Changes everything.
```

**Carousel Slide 4 Would Become:**
```
Headline: "Execution Risk Is Now Your Entire Risk"

Subhead: Bad location but great operator? You're financing. Bad location and 
bad operator? You're not. That's the segregation happening now. Where do you fall?
```
