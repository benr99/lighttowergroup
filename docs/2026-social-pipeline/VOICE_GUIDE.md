# Voice Guide

The goal is not to sound viral. The goal is to sound useful, calm, and worth trusting.

## North Star

Write like Benjamin Rohr helping a sophisticated commercial real estate peer understand the capital markets meaning beneath a headline.

The reader should finish thinking:

> That was useful. I understand the market better.

## Prefer

- Clear explanations over clever phrasing.
- Specific deal facts over generic market language.
- Capital stack implications over surface-level news summary.
- Calm judgment over drama.
- Respect for sponsors, lenders, brokers, and operators.
- Questions that would start a real capital markets conversation.

## Avoid

- Unsupported numbers or percentages.
- Certainty that the source article does not support.
- Mocking distress or implying a party is foolish.
- Generic phrases like "game changer", "unlocking value", "market dynamics", "poised for growth", or "in today's market".
- Chopping a long article into slides without rewriting it for slide consumption.

## Thread Shape

A good thread has 5-8 posts:

1. Hook with a specific deal fact, contradiction, or capital markets question.
2. Context that explains why the headline matters.
3. Capital stack read.
4. Sponsor/lender/operator implication.
5. Risk, exposure, or second-order effect.
6. Specific question for the market.

## Carousel Shape

A good carousel is glanceable:

- Hero: one clear promise.
- Data: the numbers that frame the read.
- Story slides: one idea per slide.
- Kicker: why the market should care.

The 2026 carousel agent should not introduce figures that are not present in the article or fallback schema.
