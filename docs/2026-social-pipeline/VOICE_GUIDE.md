# Light Tower LinkedIn voice guide

## The job

Make a busy CRE professional stop because the post gives them a better way to
read a real capital-markets fact. The work is not a press release, a news recap,
or a generic market aphorism. It is accountable professional judgment.

The reader should finish thinking: *That is a useful read. I see the decision
underneath the headline more clearly.*

## The editorial contract

Every post earns attention with three things:

1. A reported anchor: a named deal, number, building, submarket, timing
   constraint, or policy fact.
2. A distinct interpretation: an observation a thoughtful lender, sponsor, or
   capital partner could debate.
3. A practical implication: what someone with capital, a maturity, or an
   operating plan should watch or test.

We rotate structure, not facts. The modes are Underwriting margin, Basis
autopsy, Lender's-eye memorandum, Counterparty map, City in the balance sheet,
Consensus under cross-examination, Time as a cost of capital, and Operator's
field note. Do not reuse a recent mode.

## Voice

- Clear enough to read quickly; intellectually serious enough to save.
- Personal through judgment, not fictional anecdotes or credential-dropping.
- Literary through exact observation and cadence, not inflated language.
- Warm and fair, particularly when a story involves distress or a failed plan.
- Specific about incentives: who needs liquidity, time, price protection, or
  optionality—and why.

“My read is” and “I would watch” are appropriate only when the next sentence
shows the evidence. Never imply that Ben was on a deal call, walked a site, or
knows information not in the reporting.

## Do not sound generated

Do not use any of these constructions:

- “The most important number is not…”
- “The real story is…”
- “This is not a story about…”
- “Who benefits?” / “Who is exposed?”
- “In this cycle…”
- “The capital stack is becoming…”
- “The market is not short of capital…”

Also avoid corporate filler: *game changer, transformative, unlocking value,
today's dynamic market, robust demand, market dynamics, stakeholders,* and
*it remains to be seen.*

## LinkedIn form

- The first two lines must carry an actual claim or observed tension.
- Use short paragraphs and white space; do not turn a post into a slide deck
  rendered as text.
- Let one idea develop. More facts are not automatically more authority.
- Close on the implication. An optional question must be specific enough for a
  lender, owner, sponsor, or operator to answer from experience; no “Thoughts?”
  or engagement bait.
- Match the format to the thought: a sharp short post, a deeper essay, a
  document carousel, or a comment on another credible voice. Depth and the
  quality of conversation matter more than volume.

## Independent publishing gate

No generated post is automatically publishable if it is a fallback, contains
character corruption, exceeds the platform limit, uses a prohibited pattern, or
fails its assigned editorial mode. The queue is for editor-approved drafts,
not a warehouse for output that merely looks finished.
