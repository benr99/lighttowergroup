Frequently Asked Questions

What is a commercial real estate recapitalization?+
A recapitalization restructures the ownership and/or debt of an existing asset. This typically involves refinancing debt, bringing in new equity partners, buying out partners, or restructuring the capital stack. Recaps are common when sponsors have created value but need to restructure their capital to extract equity, extend hold periods, or bring in capital for the next phase.
When should a sponsor consider an investment advisor?+
Sponsors benefit from an advisor when facing complex capital decisions: structuring a multi-tranche capital stack, evaluating whether to sell or recapitalize, optimizing portfolio-level capital allocation, or navigating opportunities that require speed and expertise. An advisor provides objective analysis, broader market intelligence, and access to more capital sources than most sponsors reach independently.
