Equity Placement

Connecting sponsors with institutional and family office capital. We structure and source JV equity, preferred equity, and Co-GP capital for acquisitions, developments, and recapitalizations.

Joint Venture Equity

For sponsors seeking a capital partner to co-invest in a transaction. We structure JV partnerships that align incentives between operator and investor through carefully negotiated waterfall structures, promote tiers, and governance provisions.

Our equity sourcing process reaches institutional investors, family offices, and private equity real estate funds actively deploying capital. We create competitive tension among equity sources to ensure the sponsor secures the best available terms â€” maximizing promote, minimizing co-invest requirements, and preserving operational control.

Preferred Equity

Subordinate capital that fills the gap between senior debt and common equity. Preferred equity provides additional leverage for sponsors who want to reduce their equity contribution while maintaining full upside participation in the common equity position.

We source preferred equity from specialized funds, insurance companies, and private credit providers at rates and terms that enhance sponsor returns without creating the restrictive covenants associated with mezzanine debt structures.

Co-GP Capital

For sponsors who need help meeting the GP co-investment requirement in a joint venture. Co-GP capital reduces the sponsor's out-of-pocket equity commitment while allowing them to maintain their operating role and promote structure.

We connect emerging and established sponsors with Co-GP investors who understand real estate operations and provide patient, partnership-oriented capital.

Family Office Capital

Direct investment relationships with family offices seeking commercial real estate exposure. Family offices often offer more flexible terms, faster decision-making, and longer hold periods than institutional investors.

Our family office network spans domestic and international capital sources actively deploying into US commercial real estate across asset types and geographies.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is JV equity in commercial real estate?+
Joint venture (JV) equity is a partnership structure where an operating partner (the sponsor) partners with a capital partner to acquire, develop, or recapitalize a property. The capital partner typically contributes 85â€“95% of the equity while the sponsor earns a promoted interest above specified return hurdles.
What is the difference between preferred equity and JV equity?+
Preferred equity sits between senior debt and common equity in the capital stack, receiving a fixed return before common equity holders. JV equity participates in the full upside through a waterfall structure. Preferred equity is lower risk but capped return; JV equity has higher risk with potential for much higher returns.
